LESSON 6.01
SATB Voice Leading in Minor

📖 TODAY'S FOCUS
When four voices sing together, why does it matter exactly how each one moves from note to note?
SATB voice leading is one of the most precise skills in tonal theory. In minor, there is one extra concern beyond the standard rules: the raised leading tone in harmonic minor must resolve upward by half step. Get the rules right and four independent voices blend into a single coherent harmony.

🎵 PART 1 · THE SATB GRAND STAFF

SATB stands for Soprano, Alto, Tenor, Bass, the four standard choral voices. In written exercises, Soprano and Alto share the treble staff (stems up for S, down for A); Tenor and Bass share the bass staff (stems up for T, down for B). Each voice stays within its own range.

🎼 SATB VOCAL RANGES (OMT p. 186)
Soprano
E4 to G5
Alto
C4 to E5
Tenor
C3 to G4
Bass
E2 to C4
🎶 PART 2 · THE VOICE-LEADING RULES CARD

These rules apply whenever you write SATB texture, whether in major or minor. Minor adds one extra rule about the leading tone.

SATB VOICE-LEADING RULES
1
Move each voice as little as possible.
Prefer common tones (stay on the same pitch) or steps. Leaps are allowed but should be rare and smooth.
2
Prefer contrary and oblique motion.
When the bass moves one direction, the upper voices ideally move the other direction (contrary) or stay (oblique). Similar motion is weaker; parallel motion is risky.
3
No parallel fifths or parallel octaves.
Two voices cannot both move in the same direction and arrive at a perfect 5th (or perfect 8th/octave) consecutively. These erase voice independence.
4
No voice crossing.
The soprano must always be higher than the alto; the alto higher than the tenor; the tenor higher than the bass. Voices must not leapfrog each other.
5
Double the root in root-position chords.
In a four-voice texture one note of each chord is duplicated. Default: double the root (the bass note). This stabilizes the harmony.
6
Never double the leading tone.
The leading tone (scale degree 7, raised in harmonic minor) has a strong pull to resolve. Doubling it forces two voices into the same resolution and creates parallel octaves. Avoid it.
7
Minor extra: leading tone must resolve up by half step.
In harmonic minor, scale degree 7 (G♯ in A minor) must rise by half step to the tonic (A). Never let it skip down or stay in place.
🎹 PART 3 · PARALLEL FIFTHS = FORBIDDEN

Parallel fifths sound hollow and erase the sense that two independent voices are moving. The rule is absolute in classical tonal writing.

⚠ AUGMENTED 2ND AVOIDANCE (minor extra)
Do not write a melodic augmented 2nd (from natural scale degree 6 to raised scale degree 7) in any single voice. If a voice must pass through both scale degrees 6 and 7, use melodic minor (raise degree 6 as well) to make it a smooth whole step.

💪 THE ONE THING TO REMEMBER

In SATB voice leading, every voice moves as little as possible, and no two voices arrive at a perfect 5th or octave by moving the same direction. In minor, there is one additional hard rule: the raised leading tone (scale degree 7) must resolve upward by half step to the tonic, every time, no exceptions.
