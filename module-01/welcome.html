Course Orientation
🎵 WELCOME BACK
You already know the fundamentals. Now you go further.
If you completed Music Theory 1, you spent a year learning the grammar of music: how notes are named and written, how scales and key signatures work, how intervals are built, how triads are constructed, and how Roman numerals describe harmonic motion. That is a real foundation.
Music Theory 2 picks up exactly where you left off. This year you will push into territory that working musicians, composers, and arrangers use every day: minor keys in all their forms, the ancient modes, richer chords, counterpoint, classical forms, jazz harmony, world music, and a capstone composition of your own. The tools you already have will carry you here. The new ones will change how you hear everything.
🎯 WHAT YOU WILL BUILD THIS YEAR
Theory II has seven major areas, each building on the last:
1
Minor keys in all three scale forms
2
Modes and modal mixture (borrowed chords)
3
Seventh chords and their inversions
4
Counterpoint: two voices in conversation
5
Classical forms: binary, ternary, sonata, rondo
6
Jazz and world music harmony and rhythm
7
Advanced composition capstone: a full original piece applying everything

📚 HOW THIS COURSE WORKS
Music Theory 2 is fully self-paced. You decide when you work each week, as long as you keep up with the module pace. Here is the rhythm:
▶
One module per week (~80 minutes total). No fixed meeting times.
▶
Interactive lessons are on GitHub and embedded in Canvas. Work through them at your own pace before doing Canvas assignments.
▶
Open Music Theory v2 is your free textbook. Every reading is linked directly in the lesson. No purchase needed.
▶
MuseScore (free) is your notation software for composition work. Download it at musescore.org if you do not already have it.
▶
Questions? Use Canvas messaging to reach your instructor. Optional ungraded office hours are posted in Canvas each week - check the Announcements.

⚡ HOW TO SUCCEED IN THIS COURSE
Theory 2 is more demanding than Theory 1 - that is expected, and it is manageable. Three habits make the difference:
1. Work a little every day rather than all at once.
Ear training and notation recognition are motor skills. Short daily sessions beat one long session. Even 10 minutes counts.
2. Use the OMT reading before the lesson, not after.
Each lesson links the exact pages. Read first, then work through the lesson. The concepts click faster when you have seen the vocabulary.
3. Ask early, not the night before something is due.
Canvas messaging reaches your instructor. Peers in the discussion boards are also a resource. Theory gets easier when you talk through it.
💪 READY TO BEGIN

When you are ready, head to Lesson 1.01 for a Theory I refresher. You will review the fundamentals that carry you through all of Theory 2 and take a self-assessment to identify where you want to strengthen your skills before new content starts in Module 2.
